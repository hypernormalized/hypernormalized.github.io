A Quiet Corner

2026·02·18

In 1986, Hiroshi Yoshimura released Green. Where Nine Post Cards felt solitary—music for an empty museum—Green feels alive. He recorded it using crisp FM synthesis (digital), yet the result feels incredibly organic. He called it “music that drifts like smoke.”

This sketch attempts a visual translation of that paradox: using cold logic to render warmth. Soft, translucent forms overlap and breathe. No hard edges. The colors shift almost imperceptibly, like light changing in a room over an afternoon.

It’s digital moss growing on a screen. A small corner of the internet where nothing happens quickly.
